# Key Research Questions: Novel Cancer Curing Algorithm

Based on the defined scope, the initial research will focus on answering the following key questions:

1.  **Current Landscape:** What are the most promising cutting-edge computational or algorithmic approaches currently being researched for cancer treatment (e.g., AI/ML in drug discovery, personalized therapy algorithms, predictive modeling)?
2.  **AI/ML Applications:** How are AI and machine learning specifically being used to develop novel cancer therapies or diagnostic algorithms? Are there specific algorithms (e.g., deep learning, reinforcement learning) showing significant promise?
3.  **Personalized Medicine:** What novel algorithms are being developed to personalize cancer treatment based on individual patient data (genomics, proteomics, imaging, clinical history)?
4.  **Drug Discovery & Repurposing:** Are there new algorithms accelerating the discovery of novel anti-cancer drugs or identifying existing drugs that can be repurposed for cancer treatment?
5.  **Predictive Modeling:** What algorithms are emerging for predicting patient response to specific cancer therapies (e.g., immunotherapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy)?
6.  **Systems Biology & Networks:** How are computational models of biological systems and networks being used to identify new therapeutic targets or understand cancer progression algorithmically?
7.  **Immunotherapy Optimization:** Are there algorithms being developed to optimize immunotherapy strategies, such as predicting neoantigens or patient response to checkpoint inhibitors?
8.  **Data Integration:** What algorithmic approaches are being used to integrate multi-omics data (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics) for a more comprehensive understanding and treatment of cancer?
9.  **Emerging Technologies:** Are there novel computational paradigms (e.g., quantum computing, advanced simulations) being explored for cancer research algorithms?
10. **Key Researchers/Institutions:** Who are the leading researchers, institutions, or companies pioneering these novel algorithmic approaches in cancer research?
